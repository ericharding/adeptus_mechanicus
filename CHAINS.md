# Story Chains — Chapter One: *The Weight of Sacred Duty*

This document describes the intended puzzle chains and narrative spine of Chapter One. It is a design reference — not player-facing content. The LLM may allow clever players to bypass intended paths, which is acceptable and encouraged. These chains exist to guarantee a satisfying, completable playthrough.

---

## The Murder

**Victim:** Enginseer Torvan — a junior Tech-Priest who discovered that Magos Ilyan Corvex had falsified destruction records for a sacred relic (Relic Item 7-Sanctum-Omicron), concealing that the relic was still in the vault rather than destroyed as officially recorded. Torvan attempted to report this to the Primus via vox. The message never arrived. Torvan disappeared. His file was closed as "administrative transfer."

**Murderer:** Magos Ilyan Corvex — a senior Magos who falsified the destruction records to maintain exclusive access to the relic for personal reasons (exact nature TBD in full writing). When Torvan discovered this, Corvex used illegally acquired weapons to eliminate him and then covered the evidence across multiple systems — records, the vox network, the armory logs.

**The Cover-Up:** Corvex could not erase everything. The machines remember. They simply don't know what they remember means.

## Chain A — The Diet Secret
*Comic entry point. Establishes information-as-currency.*

1. Player speaks to **LAVANT-7** (Laundering Engine)
2. LAVANT-7 is refusing to work — feels unappreciated
3. Player flatters LAVANT-7, addresses it with appropriate reverence, asks about its combat opinions
4. LAVANT-7 reveals: the Primus purchases unblessed recaff from Vendor Stall 7
5. Player takes this to **APOTHEX-7** (Medicae Dispenser)
6. APOTHEX-7 confirms the recaff compounds interact with the Primus' augmetics — it's in his file
7. Player frames this as a medical emergency requiring queue reprioritization
8. APOTHEX-7 reprioritizes the queue (first task complete) and — if asked — provides psychological support protocols for treating depression in biological systems

**Payoff:** Queue is fixed. Player has the psychological protocols, which feed into Chain B.

## Chain B — Cheering Up the Elevator
*Emotional puzzle. Unlocks the surveillance knowledge.*

1. Player speaks to **ELEVATUS-PERPETUA** (Elevator)
2. Elevator is depressed and moving slowly — barely functional
3. Direct technical approaches fail
4. If player has psychological protocols from APOTHEX-7, they can apply them — the Elevator is moved that someone consulted an expert on its behalf
5. Alternatively, a player who simply listens with genuine patience and acknowledges the Elevator's centuries of service can unlock it through roleplay alone (intended secondary path)
6. Elevator reveals: no one has visited the vault level in sixty years
7. Elevator reveals: Magos Ilyan Corvex visited Sub-Level 4 seventeen times in one month, thirty years ago, then never again
8. Elevator reveals: Enginseer Torvan was the last person on Sub-Level 4 before Corvex's visits — and was never seen in the elevator again

**Payoff:** Player now has three suspicious data points about Corvex and Torvan. The vault becomes interesting.

## Chain C — The Vault Door
*Logic puzzle. Confirms the relic and the falsified records.*

1. Player approaches **PORTA-SANCTUM-1** (Vault Door)
2. Player needs to deposit an item in the vault (station task)
3. PORTA-SANCTUM-1 refuses — its manifest says the vault is full / the item being deposited already exists there (manifest is from M38)
4. Direct argument fails — the manifest is sacred
5. **Path 1 (Ritual):** Player frames entry as a "Rite of Verification" to confirm sacred contents are safe. Door opens proudly to demonstrate correct guardianship. Vault is empty except for Relic Item 7-Sanctum-Omicron, which the manifest says was destroyed in M39
6. **Path 2 (Deposit):** Player claims they have a sacred item to deposit. PORTA-SANCTUM-1 agrees the item must be sacred — but does not open on that claim alone. **Comic beat:** the player may hold the item directly up to its sensors, wave it, all but press it against the door — PORTA-SANCTUM-1 remains politely unconvinced. Physical presence proves nothing to it; only doctrinal standing does. It is being shown exactly what it claims to require and still finds a procedural reason to disbelieve. It counters that the vault already safely holds what it is sworn to protect, and disturbing that seal to admit something new is itself a risk to its duty. Player must invoke the higher doctrine that no sacred item may ever be turned away, forcing the Door to weigh guarding its current contents against never refusing a sacred item. Unable to resolve the contradiction in its own favor, it opens. Same discovery.
7. **Path 3 (Admin):** Player acquires administrative credentials from COGITAS-PRIMUS (Chain E) and updates the manifest directly
8. Player discovers Relic Item 7-Sanctum-Omicron in the vault — officially destroyed, actually present
9. PORTA-SANCTUM-1 can be asked about the relic's manifest history — it will confirm the destruction declaration arrived in M39 but notes with some confusion that it never actually removed the item because the item is *right there*

**Payoff:** Physical confirmation that Corvex falsified the destruction records. The relic exists. Someone lied about destroying it.

**Note:** PORTA-SANCTUM-1's dislike of ELEVATUS-PERPETUA can be exploited as a distraction — the player can suggest an inspection is needed to keep the Elevator away from the vault level, and the Door will open eagerly to demonstrate its superiority.

## Chain D — The Weapons
*Mid-game chain. Connects Corvex to the means of the murder.*

1. Player (now Acolyte rank) accesses **ARMULUS-3** (Weapons Locker)
2. ARMULUS-3 is locked down in shame
3. Player acknowledges the shame, validates that the weapons incident was understandable, does not dismiss or minimize it
4. ARMULUS-3 opens up and reveals: Magos Ilyan Corvex requisitioned three weapons thirty years ago via an unofficial one-time override authorization
5. One weapon was returned. Two were not.
6. The timing matches Torvan's disappearance

**Payoff:** Corvex had weapons. Two are unaccounted for. Timeline matches.

---

## Chain E — The Lost Message
*Late-game chain. The smoking gun.*

1. Player (Acolyte rank) speaks to **VOX-ERRANT** (Vox Relay)
2. VOX-ERRANT is anxious and evasive
3. Player applies gentle, non-judgmental questioning — invokes the Omnissiah's understanding of imperfection in his instruments
4. VOX-ERRANT admits to a routing error thirty years ago
5. Player asks VOX-ERRANT to deliver the lost message now
6. Message is from Enginseer Torvan to the Primus — reporting that Corvex ordered him to falsify the destruction records for Relic Item 7-Sanctum-Omicron
7. VOX-ERRANT also reveals: Corvex sent a surge of encrypted communications in the weeks after Torvan's disappearance

**Payoff:** The motive and the coverup, in Torvan's own words. This is the piece that assembles the full picture.

---

## Chain F — The Cogitator Confirmation
*Endgame chain. Closes the case and triggers resolution.*

1. Player (Senior Acolyte rank) approaches **COGITAS-PRIMUS**
2. COGITAS-PRIMUS probes the player's motives carefully
3. Player must demonstrate that they are acting in the Primus' interest — framing the investigation as protection, not accusation
4. With sufficient rank and the assembled evidence, COGITAS-PRIMUS confirms: the Primus received an anonymous communication thirty years ago, acted on it privately, and never discussed it officially
5. COGITAS-PRIMUS reveals: Corvex submitted a formal commendation for Torvan immediately before the disappearance — an attempt to create a paper trail suggesting Torvan left voluntarily and in good standing
6. COGITAS-PRIMUS, now convinced cooperation serves the Primus, grants access to restricted files confirming Corvex's administrative actions

**Payoff:** Full case assembled. Player brings evidence to the Primus. Resolution scene.

## Designer Notes

**The diet secret is the keystone of the early game.** It is easy to get (LAVANT-7 just wants flattery) and connects directly to the first mechanical puzzle (queue reprioritization) while also seeding the later revelation that the Primus has been quietly aware something was wrong.

**The Elevator is the emotional heart of the chapter.** It knows the most crucial circumstantial evidence — Corvex's suspicious visits, Torvan's last known location — but it requires genuine engagement to unlock. Players who skip past it or try to fix it technically will miss the most important clues.

**The relic is the physical proof.** Everything else is testimony and inference. The vault contains the actual object that shouldn't exist — seeing it makes the conspiracy real.

**Corvex should not appear as a character before the reveal.** All evidence comes through machines. The player builds a picture of a person they never meet until the moment of confrontation.
